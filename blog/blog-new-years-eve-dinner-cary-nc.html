New Year's Eve at Crawford Brothers

July 2025

Close the Year with Prime

New Year's Eve calls for a steakhouse. Not a chain. Not a gastropub. A steakhouse—where the dry-aging program runs to the bone, where the wine list speaks for itself, and where the cocktails arrive built with intention. That's Crawford Brothers in Cary.

The year closes differently when you're eating prime beef dry-aged in-house, when your glass holds something from a 300-bottle list curated by Beverage Director Jordan Joseph, and when the room itself—tucked into the Triangle's dining anchor at Fenton—feels like it was built for exactly this moment.

The Prime Cuts

Crawford Brothers doesn't do compromise on protein. Every steak is USDA Prime, aged in the glass-fronted room visible from the dining floor. The dry-aging process concentrates flavor and intensifies tenderness—the beef tastes like beef, concentrated.

The bone-in ribeye demands a dry, 140°F interior if you're serious. The New York strip takes a slight crust and the blush inside. The filet goes butter-tender without the crutch of marbling. The porterhouse is two steaks in one cut. Sides—creamed spinach, loaded baked potato, truffle fries—step back and let the beef hold the plate.

The Drinks

Jordan Joseph shapes this program with the discipline of a chef. The cocktails aren't decoration. A Gangster Room martini arrives properly stirred, properly up, properly chilled. The Sazerac uses proper rye. The old-fashioned lives on one rock.

The wine list spans continents and decades—300+ selections. Champagne for the midnight moment. Burgundy if you're thinking about the future. California Cabernet if you're thinking about the steak.

The Rooms

The Gangster Room seats six. Six people behind glass, looking out at the dry-aging program—you're dining inside the restaurant's spine. Intimate. Specific. Built for the moment you want the year to close in someone's company and no one else's.

Larger group? The PDR holds up to 24. Still private. Still yours for the night.

The Booking

New Year's Eve fills. Crawford Brothers opens at 5pm that night—every seat spoken for weeks in advance. Reserve through Tock. If you're thinking about it, book it. If you're thinking about a private room, book sooner.

Crawford Brothers lives at 401 Fenton Gateway Drive in Cary—the heart of Fenton. Open daily 5pm–10pm. Call (919) 629-4029 if you have questions a reservation can't answer.

That's how you close a year. Prime beef. Proper drinks. The room, the table, the people who matter. Book it.

Is Crawford Brothers open on New Year's Eve?

Yes. Crawford Brothers is open on New Year's Eve from 5pm–10pm. We recommend reserving well in advance as seating fills quickly for the holiday.

Is there a special New Year's Eve menu?

We serve our full menu on New Year's Eve—dry-aged prime cuts, fresh seafood, and classic steakhouse sides. Our Beverage Director Jordan Joseph's cocktail menu and 300+ wine list provide the perfect pairing for your celebration.

Can I book a private room for New Year's Eve?

Yes. The Gangster Room seats 6 guests in an intimate setting behind the dry-aging glass. The PDR accommodates groups up to 24. Book through Tock or call (919) 629-4029 to reserve.
