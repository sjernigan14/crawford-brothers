How to Pair Wine with Steak

What wine goes best with steak?

Start with a full-bodied red. Cabernet Sauvignon is the classic choice — high tannins cut through the richness of beef, and the dark fruit complements charred, seasoned meat. But the best pairing depends on the cut, the preparation, and what you actually enjoy drinking. We pour wines by the glass specifically chosen to pair with our menu. The most important rule is that there is no wrong answer if you like it.

What wine pairs with ribeye?

Ribeye is rich, well-marbled, and bold. It needs a wine that can stand up to the fat and intensity. Cabernet Sauvignon from Napa or Paso Robles. Malbec from Mendoza. Even a Barolo if you like structure and complexity. The tannins in these wines cleanse the palate between bites and let you taste the beef again, fresh.

What wine pairs with filet mignon?

Filet is leaner and more delicate than ribeye. A heavy Cabernet can overpower it. Try Pinot Noir from Willamette Valley or Burgundy. Merlot works well too. The goal is a wine with enough acidity and gentle tannin to complement the tenderness without competing with it. The wine should enhance the steak, not dominate.

What wine pairs with a New York strip?

The strip sits between ribeye and filet in terms of fat and flavor. It can handle serious reds. Cabernet is always safe. Syrah from the Rhône Valley or Washington State red blends both work beautifully. At Crawford Brothers, our sommelier can walk you through what we have by the glass and match your preferences to your meal.

Should I ask the sommelier?

Always. Our wine list is built specifically to pair with our steaks, seafood, and raw bar. The sommelier knows which bottles are drinking well right now and can match your preferences to your meal and mood. That is what they are here for. They are not there to judge what you order. They are there to make your night better.
