Mother's Day Dinner at Crawford Brothers

Mother's Day calls for a restaurant that doesn't just serve good food—one that understands occasion. Crawford Brothers, situated at Fenton in Cary, delivers exactly that. The dry-aged prime steaks arrive prepared with precision. The wine list runs 300+ bottles deep. The service moves with intention. And the space—intimate enough for the moment, refined without pretense—makes it clear that thought went into every detail.

The Wine Program

Head Sommelier Mat Brochard and Assistant Sommelier Anita Tingle have built something that goes beyond a list. Twenty-five selections pour by the glass, ranging $16–$65, which means there's genuinely something for every budget and preference. A classic Burgundy pairs differently than Oregon Pinot. A California Cabernet versus a Bordeaux blend—each tells a different story. For Mother's Day, this flexibility lets your celebration meet her actual taste, not a compromise.

The full list spans 300+ bottles. If your mother enjoys exploring wine, Brochard and Tingle offer real guidance—not shortcuts. They know what works with the kitchen's cooking. They can build a progression across multiple courses without heavy-handedness.

The Meal Itself

Dry-aged prime steaks form the backbone. The aging process concentrates flavor and changes texture—the meat becomes tender in a specific way that fresh beef cannot match. The cuts vary—ribeye, New York, filet—each with its own character.

The seafood tower offers an alternative approach—oysters, shrimp, crab—all pointing toward brightness and the sea. It's a different kind of celebration, one that feels lighter without sacrificing substance.

Tableside experiences—Caesar salads built in front of you, steak cut and plated with ceremony—bring back something that modern dining often loses: presence. Someone standing at your table, attending to the moment, isn't just service. It's acknowledgment that this dinner matters.

The Setting

The main dining room works for two people or a small group. The private dining room—seating 24—handles larger family gatherings without feeling cavernous. You can book it for the whole party, which means control over timing, privacy, and the shape of the evening on your terms.

The Practical Details

Book through Tock. Mother's Day fills quickly—reserve well in advance. The restaurant sits at 401 Fenton Gateway Dr., Cary, with parking immediate. Service runs daily 5pm–10pm.

Gift cards are available if you want to give the experience itself—let her decide the date, the dish, the wine. Call (919) 629-4029 to ask about purchasing options.
